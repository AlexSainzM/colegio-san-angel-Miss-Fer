üìñ Possessive Pronouns 10-25 min

What are Possessive Pronouns?

Possessive pronouns replace a noun. They show who owns something without repeating the noun.

With possessive adjective: This is my book.

With possessive pronoun: This book is mine. (We don't repeat "book")

Possessive Adjectives vs Possessive Pronouns

Subject Possessive Adjective Possessive Pronoun Example
ImymineThis pen is mine.
YouyouryoursIs this book yours?
HehishisThe backpack is his.
SheherhersThat phone is hers.
Ititsits(rarely used)
WeouroursThis classroom is ours.
TheytheirtheirsThose books are theirs.

üí° Remember: Possessive pronouns NEVER use an apostrophe! It's "yours" not "your's"!

Using 's to Show Possession

We add 's to a person's name to show they own something.

Maria's pencil = The pencil belongs to Maria

John's backpack = The backpack belongs to John

The teacher's book = The book belongs to the teacher

‚ùì Questions with WHOSE 10-30 min

What is "Whose"?

Whose is a question word we use to ask about ownership - who something belongs to.

Whose + noun + verb...?

"Whose book is this?" ‚Üí "It's Maria's." / "It's mine."

"Whose pencils are these?" ‚Üí "They're John's." / "They're his."

Two Ways to Use "Whose"

StructureExample QuestionExample Answer
Whose + noun Whose backpack is this? It's Ana's. / It's hers.
Whose + noun Whose books are those? They're Pedro's. / They're his.
Whose (alone) Whose is this pen? It's mine.
Whose (alone) Whose are these? They're ours.
