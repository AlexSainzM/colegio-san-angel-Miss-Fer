What are Reflexive Pronouns?

Reflexive pronouns are used when the subject and object of a verb are the same person.

Example: I see myself in the mirror.

("I" is doing the action AND receiving it = same person)

The Complete List of Reflexive Pronouns

Subject Pronoun Reflexive Pronoun Example
ImyselfI taught myself to play guitar.
You (singular)yourselfDid you hurt yourself?
HehimselfHe introduced himself to the class.
SheherselfShe bought herself a new dress.
ItitselfThe cat cleaned itself.
WeourselvesWe enjoyed ourselves at the party.
You (plural)yourselvesDid you all prepare yourselves?
TheythemselvesThey blamed themselves for the mistake.

üí° "By myself/ourselves" = alone, without help

Example: "I did my homework by myself." (= I did it alone, nobody helped me)

Julia's Independent Day

Last Saturday, Julia decided to spend the day by herself. She woke up early and made breakfast by herself. She cooked eggs and toast, and she was proud of herself.

After breakfast, Julia looked at herself in the mirror and decided to try a new hairstyle. She styled her hair by herself, without asking her mom for help.

In the afternoon, Julia taught herself some new dance moves by watching videos online. She practiced until she was tired, but she enjoyed herself a lot.

At night, Julia thought to herself: "I can do many things on my own!" She felt really happy with herself.
