üìå When do we use WILL?

  • Predictions about the future: I think it will rain tomorrow.
  • Future plans and intentions: I will study abroad next year.
  • Spontaneous decisions: I'm thirsty. I will drink some water.
  • Promises: I will help you with your homework.
  • Offers: I will carry your bag.

‚úÖ Affirmative Form (Will)

Subject + WILL + Base Verb
Examples:
‚Ä¢ I will travel to Europe next summer.
‚Ä¢ She will study medicine at university.
‚Ä¢ They will visit us next month.
‚Ä¢ We will go to the beach tomorrow.

‚ùå Negative Form (Won't / Will Not)

Examples:
‚Ä¢ I won't forget your birthday.
‚Ä¢ He won't come to the party.
‚Ä¢ They won't know the answer.
‚Ä¢ We will not accept late assignments.

‚ùì Question Form

WILL + Subject + Base Verb + ?
Examples:
‚Ä¢ Will you help me with this?
‚Ä¢ Will she arrive on time?
‚Ä¢ Will they come to the meeting?
‚Ä¢ What will you do tomorrow?

‚ö†Ô∏è Important Notes:
‚Ä¢ WILL is the same for all subjects (I, you, he, she, it, we, they)
‚Ä¢ Use the BASE FORM of the verb (no -s, no -ed, no -ing)
‚Ä¢ WON'T is the contraction of WILL NOT
‚Ä¢ DON'T use "to" after will: ‚ùå will to go ‚Üí ‚úÖ will go
