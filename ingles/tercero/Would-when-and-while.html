üìñ Grammar: Would / Would Like 10-25 min

Making Polite Requests

StructureExampleUse
Would you like + noun? Would you like some coffee? Offering something
Would you like to + verb? Would you like to come to my party? Invitations
I would like + noun I would like a glass of water, please. Polite requests
I would like to + verb I would like to ask a question. Expressing wishes

Short Forms

I would like = I'd like

Would you like = Common in questions (no short form)

Yes, I would = Yes, I'd love to

üìñ Grammar: When vs While 10-30 min

Understanding the Difference

WordUseTense Pattern
WHEN Specific moment / Interruption Past Continuous + when + Past Simple
WHILE Two actions happening at the same time While + Past Continuous, Past Continuous

Visual Examples

WHEN = Interruption ‚ö°

I was sleeping when the phone rang.

(Long action interrupted by short action)

WHILE = Simultaneous ‚áÑ

While I was cooking, my sister was watching TV.

(Two long actions at the same time)

More Examples

üìñ Using WHEN:

‚Ä¢ I was walking home when it started to rain.

‚Ä¢ She was doing homework when her mom called.

‚Ä¢ They were having dinner when the lights went out.

üìñ Using WHILE:

‚Ä¢ While I was reading, my brother was playing games.

‚Ä¢ While she was talking on the phone, he was making coffee.
