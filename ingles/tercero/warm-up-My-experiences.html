üìö Grammar Review

Part 1: Present Perfect vs. Past Simple

Visual Timeline

Past Simple

‚úÖ Specific time in the past

"I visited Paris last year."

"She ate sushi yesterday."

Present Perfect

‚úÖ Experience until now

"I have visited Paris."

"She has eaten sushi."

üîë Key Differences

Present Perfect: Focus on the experience

‚úÖ "I have seen that movie."

Past Simple: Focus on when it happened

‚úÖ "I saw that movie last week."

Part 3: Narrative Connectors

These words help us connect our ideas when telling stories about our experiences:

First

Para comenzar una historia

"First, I woke up early."

Then / After that

Para continuar la secuencia

"Then, I had breakfast."

Finally

Para terminar la historia

"Finally, I went to bed."

Because

Para dar razones

"I was happy because..."

So

Para mostrar resultados

"I was tired, so I rested."

But

Para contrastar ideas

"I was tired, but I continued."

Part 4: Complete Story Example

Read this example that uses both tenses and connectors:

I have traveled to many places in Mexico. (Present Perfect - experience)

First, I visited Oaxaca in 2020. (Past Simple - specific time)

Then, I went to Chiapas last year. (Past Simple - specific time)

I loved the nature because it was beautiful. (Past Simple + connector)

After that, I explored Yucat√°n. (Past Simple + connector)

I have learned so much about Mexican culture. (Present Perfect - experience until now)

Notice:

  • Present Perfect at the beginning and end (overall experiences)
  • Past Simple for specific events with time markers
  • Connectors to organize the story (First, Then, After that, Because)
